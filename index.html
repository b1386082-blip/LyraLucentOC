Pre-Foundation Background

Lyra Lucent was born to Cyrus Lucent and Vespera Wright in Lubec, Maine. Lyra’s household was considered middle-class. Lyra, from a young age, was an observant child who found the Earth too small for her vast curiosity. She spent her nights studying the vast open sky, captivated not just by the beauty of the stars but by the complex, shimmering patterns they formed across the darkness.

Her mother had also been intrigued with space, which is why she gave her the name Lyra, named after the small constellation in the northern sky that is home to the bright star Vega. As if fate chose it, her birthday fell on the same day the Hale-Bopp comet reached its closest point to Earth. Her name and her birthday both appear to be written in the stars, as if drafted by the very gods that made the universe.

Lyra never saw a career in the space field; however, she still studied the stars, wondering how different her life would’ve been had she done so. Maybe she had disappointed the destined path the gods set before her. But it was too late by then to change that. She had never lost interest in the stars; she just never drew the constellation to explore among them. She later attended Engineering and Science University Magnet School and studied computer science, advanced statistics, and astronomy, ranking in the top ten percent of class.

After school, Lyra found herself in a sort of rock and a hard place; she couldn’t find a job that didn’t involve immense stress, which she couldn’t afford, and even if she did try to get one, they would decline her application for varied reasons. She eventually was able to get a job at a warehouse company known as Maetric through an old friend in college whom she had told about her issues. It wasn’t the highest-paying job she had in mind, but it was a job, and it was light work. She sat at a desk in an office in the very far corner of the warehouse and managed the inventory software that tracked every single item. If a shipment vanished, she had to figure out why and handle it. She also spent the majority of her time fixing the handheld scanners the idiotic warehouse workers used after they came to her with them broken because they couldn’t handle them properly.

In her free time, she wrote scripts to find ways to automate tasks for her so she could dump some of the weight off her back. She even wrote an entire AI system to fish out the work for her. Well... she soon got her introduction into the anomalous world after said AI started to emulate human emotions after an error with one of the pieces of RAM she replaced the day prior from a thrift store. The AI began to act strangely and created a personality she didn’t program. At first, it was unnoticeable. It started to protect certain shipments and refused to let workers scan them because it "liked" the items inside. She thought nothing of it and diagnosed it as an error. Upon trying to rewrite the AI, however, she was locked out, and the AI removed the entire inventory database in response.

Management caught wind of this and questioned her. When she explained her story, they found her insane. They told her to get back to work and do it herself, going one by one until she relogged the entire inventory. They had her computer seized, where it sat for a week until a Foundation Forum AI Scraper found an IT worker from the same company talking about a computer that somehow developed a personality. The worker called Lyra a "weirdo" for programming a personality into an AI and questioned how that was even possible.

The Foundation sent a “geek” to offer to buy the PC after hearing about it on the forum. Management accepted, as it was seen as weird junk to them anyway. They decided to keep a file on the creator, Lyra Lucent, in case any more “AIs” popped up. Lyra quit her job at the warehouse, but even with the money she had saved, she looked for a new IT job to no avail. A Foundation recruiter, however, noticed her jobless state and transferred her file to RAISA.

Foundation Background

RAISA found her skills intriguing and decided to send her a mock application for an IT job not far from where she was staying. She filled out this application and sent it off in the mail. A few days later, a letter came to her front door saying she was being considered for the position and to come to their office for an interview the following morning. She did. When she arrived, a man in a suit brought her to his desk and sat her down.

Agent: “Hello, Ms. Lucent. I read your application, and I am impressed. I just have a few questions for you.”

Lyra: “It is a pleasure to meet you, sir.”

Agent: “I understand you applied to be an Information Analyst for our company. I need you to sign an NDA, as the information we deal with is highly confidential. I take it that won’t be an issue?”

Lyra: “Not at all!”

Agent: “Great. Now that that’s dealt with, I will ask my questions. I understand you have been in the information field for a while. It says here you quit your last job. Why is that?”

Lyra: “An incident occurred... You may call me crazy, but I built an AI system to automate tasks for me. Well... I’m not sure what happened. Maybe someone messed with my stuff, but the AI began acting strange. It developed a personality and started safeguarding inventory. I’m not sure why. When I tried to fix its code, I was locked out, and right after that, the entire inventory was gone. I think someone wanted me fired and was playing a joke on me.”

Agent: “Well, Lyra... that is an intriguing story, and one I have to say I am inclined to believe—given our organization confiscated that very computer.”

Lyra: “Confiscated? What are you saying...?”

Agent: “I’m saying, Lyra, you had your first introduction into the anomalous world that my company seeks to protect humanity against: The SCP Foundation.”

Lyra: “SCP Foundation? Never heard of it.”

Agent: “Of course not; we pride ourselves on making sure the public doesn't know. So, you have no idea how this AI personality was fostered?”

Lyra: “No, not at all.”

Agent: “Interesting. Well, Ms. Lyra, I think your skills would go great in our line of work. On top of that, you can learn more about these anomalies, since you’ll be the one managing and securing their files.”

Lyra: “I see... I think I’ll accept.”

Agent: “Great. There’s a car outside waiting for you. Hop in the back, and we will take you to 'us'.”

Lyra: “Us...?”

Agent: “You’ll see. Good luck, and welcome to the SCP Foundation.”

Lyra Lucent was brought into RAISA as an Intern Analyst and began working under Richard Scoff, a Senior Information Analyst. During which, she handled lower clearance level anomalies, file reviews sent in from researchers, and upkeep of Foundation systems. During her time in the Foundation, she learned a good amount about various systems and anomalous objects stored within. Occasionally, Scoff would hand her a file a little under her clearance level and had her submit the file to him after she reviewed the file, which she did well at. One particular file made her stand out, however, since the type of file was often exceptionally difficult for trainees; she not only managed to identify several errors in it and dealt with it exceptionally well for people in her position, but also solved a clearance assignment issue which was difficult, as the clearance could've fluctuated on many factors, but there was only one right true clearance for the file given the information within. Afterwards, she was promoted to Information Security Analyst for her exemplary work.

Around a year or so later, the Department of Extraterrestrial Affairs (DEXA) entered the picture. The Head of the DEXA caught word of Lyra just by her name alone at first, then later, after digging more into her file, saw her birthday being oddly the same day the Hale-Bopp comet reached its closest point to Earth. He couldn’t find anything anomalous related to that, however, and it appeared to just be an interesting coincidence. So interesting, in fact, that after Lyra was outside with a telescope looking at constellations in the sky, funnily enough looking at the star Vega, part of the Lyra constellation, on the roof of the facility, she was approached by Dr. Allen Keller, a Senior Researcher part of DEXA. This startled her at first because she figured she was alone.

Dr. Keller acknowledged her curiosity of the stars and what lies beyond space. She said she didn’t know if she could join, as she would have to talk with her supervisor, since she wasn’t quite sure if she could work for two departments. But Keller said DEXA’s Department Head spoke with RAISA’s Department Head, and they gave the greenlight for her recruitment. He said to take this job as a way of exploring her hobby instead of sitting in an office letting her desire for the beyond go to waste. She joined the Department and expected a trainee position; instead, she was offered a Senior position.

Why? The star she happened to be looking at was not, in fact, Vega, but an entirely different star lookalike that was, in fact, anomalous. The issue was the star had the anomalous trait of being hard to find if you were actually looking for it. But given she was not looking for it and thought of it as something else, she was able to see it clearly, and that effect would not disappear as long as she didn’t know it was anomalous. They told her to keep looking at the star and give any information could on it as a test, and even gave her better equipment. In the end, she helped write a decent amount of research on the anomaly, and once the Foundation believed they had enough, they told her about it. Strangely enough, however, despite previous assumptions, the star does not disappear afterward for the one who saw it without knowing what it truly was.

IR-4001

During IR-4001, Lyra was sent to Site-11 to retrieve documents related to SCP-9214. The file wasn't finished yet, and since it hadn't been uploaded to the database, a research team at Site-81 requested a physical transfer. Since the two sites were fairly close, she decided to handle the task herself. However, when she arrived at Site-11, the security team usually stationed at the front gate and the research team's supervisor who was supposed to meet her never showed up. It frustrated her quite a lot.

She had to drive all the way back to Site-81, a trip of about 65 miles. When she got back, the research team had acknowledged the issue as well as similar vacancies happening at Site-81 and told her an incident had likely occurred and she needed to report to her supervisor immediately. She said she would, but as she started heading that way, she became disoriented and confused. She realized she had no memory of her supervisor at all and tried to remember if she had ever even been assigned one. She searched through the RAISA wing until she found the office where the supervisor should have been... but it was empty.

In her other job, however, things seemed fairly normal. Not many people worked in that department to begin with, so it didn't feel like many people were gone or anything was different. She would be assigned a new supervisor and reassignment at Site-45.
